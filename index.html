EAGLE
Eagles are large birds of prey known for their powerful build, sharp eyesight, and majestic flight. They belong to the family Accipitridae and are found on nearly every continent, with more than 60 species worldwide. Eagles are renowned for their sharp, hooked beaks, strong talons, and keen vision, which allows them to spot prey from great distances.

OWL
Owls are nocturnal birds of prey known for their distinctive appearance and hunting skills. They have large, forward-facing eyes that provide excellent night vision, a flat face with a disk-shaped structure that helps direct sound to their ears, and sharp talons for catching prey. Owls are silent fliers, thanks to their specially adapted feathers that muffle the sound of their wings, allowing them to sneak up on prey like small mammals, insects, and other birds.

CROW
Crows are highly intelligent and adaptable birds belonging to the genus Corvus, which also includes ravens, rooks, and jackdaws. Known for their all-black feathers, crows have strong, stout beaks and piercing eyes. They are often found in a variety of environments, including urban areas, forests, and farmlands, and are native to many parts of the world.

BUTTERFLY
Butterflies are colorful and delicate insects belonging to the order Lepidoptera, which also includes moths. They are known for their vibrant, often intricate wing patterns and their graceful flight. Butterflies undergo a complete metamorphosis, starting from eggs, developing into larvae (caterpillars), then pupating into chrysalises, and finally emerging as adult butterflies.

KINGFISHER
Kingfishers are strikingly colorful birds belonging to the family Alcedinidae, known for their vivid plumage and expert fishing skills. They are typically found near freshwater bodies like rivers, lakes, and ponds, where they hunt for fish, insects, and small aquatic creatures.

PARROT
Parrots are a diverse group of colorful, intelligent birds belonging to the order Psittaciformes, which includes more than 400 species. They are mostly found in tropical and subtropical regions, including Central and South America, Africa, Asia, and Australia. Parrots are known for their brightly colored feathers, which can range from green, red, blue, yellow, to multicolored patterns. Some of the most popular species include the Macaw, Cockatiel, African Grey, and Budgerigar.

HERON
Herons are large wading birds known for their long legs, necks, and sharp beaks, which make them excellent hunters in wetlands and shallow waters. They belong to the family Ardeidae and are found on every continent except Antarctica. Herons have a graceful and slow-moving hunting style, often standing still or walking slowly to ambush prey such as fish, frogs, and small aquatic animals.
